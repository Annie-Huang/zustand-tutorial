React context Key Considerations:
Performance: Every component consuming a context will automatically re-render whenever the provider's value changes. It is often recommended to memoize context values to prevent unnecessary re-renders.
Complexity: For very complex global state management, external libraries like Redux or Zustand may be more efficient than using Context alone.

When you use context inside of React, every single time you access your context through
that hook of useContext, or whatever custom hook you created, every time that context changes,
no matter what changes in the context, even if you're not using it, it'll re-render every single
component accessing that data.

So in our case, When our count changes, our display obviously updates, but all of our controls
(e.g <CountControls> component), which don't have anything to do with our display at all, are
also re-rendering. This can lead to massive performance problems because if you have a context
that's wrapping most of your application and you use it in lots of different places, it could cause your
entire application to re-render every time even small minor pieces of data change that only affect
1% of your application. Also, context in general just becomes hard to scale as your application
grows in size. And this is where Zustand really comes in.
